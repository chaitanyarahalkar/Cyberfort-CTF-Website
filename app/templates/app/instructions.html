General Rules and Instructions
  • Every time you start the CTF, 45 minutes will be alloted to complete the challenges.
  • Each question has some points to it depending upon the difficulty. Taking a hint will deduce 10% of the points associated with the question.
  • There is no particular order of solving the questions.
  • Judging of the round will be based on two parameters. The first is the score of the team and second is the time required. Teams having same score will be ranked by the time taken to solve the challenges.
  • Ranks can be viewed on the leaderboard page. The leaderboard time is updated everytime a submission is made.
  • Flags found are of the format cyberfort_CTF{some_text}. Some of the files may contain just the inner text within the brackets. Make sure you submit it in the format specified. Flag text is not case sensitive.
  • Incase your team doesn't have a laptop, the computer you are playing on has a kali-linux terminal environment setup for your needs. Feel free to install any extra tools you require.
  • This is a competitive environment with the aim of learning cyber security and ethical hacking. Please do not share the flags & solutions with others.
  • Internet access is granted. Feel free to explore and read about the concept behind the problem.
Special Instructions
  • Performing denial of service attacks on the server will lead to disqualification. Teams are requested to play ethically.
  • Brute force attacks on the flag form is prohibited.
  • We tried making this site pretty secure. It still may have vulnerabilites. If you tend to find one, feel free to use your superior advantage for your benefit.
  • Do not close your browser session while playing. All your information is recorded in the session.
